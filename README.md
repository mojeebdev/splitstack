# SplitStack 💰

> **Know exactly where every dollar goes — before you touch it.**

---

## The Origin

SplitStack wasn't planned. It was born from a real moment.

I was broke. Again. And the truth hit me harder than usual — I wasn't broke because I didn't make money. I was broke because every naira I made went straight to needs, wants, and everything in between. No structure. No ceiling. No capital building up for when an opportunity showed.

That pattern stops today.

So I built SplitStack — not as a product first, but as a tool for myself. A dead-simple system that forces you to split your income the moment it lands, before your hands touch it. Three buckets. Clear rules. No excuses.

If it helps me, it'll help someone else. So here it is.

---

## What It Does

SplitStack is a single-page income allocation tool. You enter what you earned, set your split percentages, and instantly see exactly how much goes to each bucket.

**The 3 Buckets:**

- 🔒 **Capital Reserve** — your war chest. Locked for investment opportunities only. This is how you stop being the person who watches opportunities pass.
- 🏠 **Essentials** — food, data, transport, bills. Non-negotiable. Protected.
- 👕 **Lifestyle** — clothes, treats, extras. Enjoy it guilt-free, but when it's empty, it's done.

**The default split:** 25% / 55% / 20% — adjustable with live sliders.

---

## Why It's Useful

Most budgeting tools are overcomplicated. Spreadsheets. Categories. Monthly reviews. Nobody follows through.

SplitStack is different:

- **Zero friction.** One number in, three numbers out.
- **Works for any income** — salary, freelance, per-project, irregular. Doesn't matter.
- **Multi-currency** — USD, NGN, EUR, GBP, GHS, KES and more.
- **The habit is the feature.** Split first, spend after. That single discipline, repeated every time income hits, is what changes your financial life over 12 months.

## The Philosophy

> "It's not how much money you make, but how much money you keep, how hard it works for you, and how many generations you keep it for."
> — Robert Kiyosaki, Rich Dad Poor Dad

Saving in naira is saving a shrinking asset. The capital bucket is designed to be moved into USD — held in Grey, Busha, or Chipper Cash — so it's protected from depreciation and ready when a real opportunity shows up.

**Naira is for spending. Dollars are for keeping.**
